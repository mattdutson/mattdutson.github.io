Summary

Since 2018 I've been a student in the computer science MS/PhD program at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. My interests are broadly in computer vision, graphics, and machine learning. During the 2018-2019 academic year I was jointly advised by Jignesh Patel and Kevin Eliceiri. Since fall 2019 I have been advised by Mohit Gupta.

In 2018 I graduated magnum cum laude from the University of Utah with an honors B.S. in physics. The topic of my thesis was Reconstruction of Cosmic Ray Geometry Using Cherenkov Backscattering, supervised by Douglas Bergman.

As far as specific technologies, I have the most experience with Python, C++, Java, Rust, UNIX, Git, NumPy, and TensorFlow. I also have some familiarity with C, C#, Perl, JavaScript, MATLAB, CUDA, OpenMP, MPI, and PyTorch.

Aside from the enjoyment I derive from its practice, my enthusiasm for computer science comes from its potential to improve the human condition. Unfortunately, technology can also concentrate power and threaten democratic institutions. I choose to conduct my work in a way that aligns with principles of democracy, privacy, sustainability, and respect for human rights.
